# NomadGao Poster Studio — Community Manager Guide

A simple tool that turns **one weekly form** into a full set of on-brand event
creatives for **NomadGao × The Hotpot House, Dharamkot** — a weekly calendar
plus a poster and story for every event, with a ready-made caption prompt.

**Live app:** https://posterstudio-one.vercel.app

> You don't need any design or tech skills. Fill the form, download the images,
> copy the caption prompt. That's it.

---

## 1. Sign in

1. Open **https://posterstudio-one.vercel.app**.
2. Click **Continue with Google** and sign in with your **NomadGao email**.

Only approved team emails can get in. If you see "access denied," ask the admin
to add your email.

---

## 2. The 3-minute flow (overview)

1. **Plan the week** — type in this week's events.
2. **Generate the week** — the app makes all the images.
3. For each event you get: **a square image (1:1)** + **a story image (9:16)** +
   **a caption prompt**.
4. **Download the images** and **copy the caption prompt** to write the caption.
5. Post on Instagram. Done.

---

## 3. Fill in the weekly form ("Plan the week")

### The week
- **Week starts / Week ends** — pick the date range (used on the calendar).
- **Theme** — the colour family. You can leave it; you can also reshuffle the
  look later in the studio.

### Each event
Click **＋ Add event** for as many events as you have. For each one:

| Field | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Event name** | e.g. "Sip & Paint Sunset" |
| **Event type** | Pick from the list. Not there? Choose **＋ Add new type…**, type the name, and it's added (an emoji is picked automatically). New types also get a photo folder in the Image Library. |
| **Date** | Pick the day. |
| **Time** | Choose from the dropdown (30-minute slots, e.g. 6:00 PM). |
| **Location** | Choose the venue: **The Hotpot House, NomadGao Rooftop, Lower Dharamkot** or **NomadGao Backyard, Lower Dharamkot**. |
| **Price** | Type the price (e.g. ₹600), or tick **Free**. |
| **Short description** | One line about the event. **This matters most** — the better your description, the better the caption. e.g. "Brushes, canvas and a sundowner." |

When everything's in, click **Generate the week →**.

> 💡 The **description** is the single biggest lever on caption quality. Write a
> vivid one-liner about what people will actually do.

---

## 4. The Poster Studio (your output)

After generating, you land in the studio. For the **weekly calendar** and for
**each event** you get one block with two things:

**1) Images**
- **SQUARE · 1:1** — for the Instagram feed (1080 × 1080).
- **STORY · 9:16** — for Instagram/WhatsApp stories (1080 × 1920).
- Click **⤓ Download PNG** under each to save it.

**2) A caption prompt**
- A ready-made prompt with the event details baked in.
- Click **📋 Copy prompt** (see the next section to turn it into a caption).

**Other buttons up top:**
- **🎲 Shuffle theme** — instantly reshuffles the colours, fonts and texture
  (always on-brand). Keep clicking until you like it, then download.
- **← Edit the week** — go back and change any details.

> Your work sticks around: switching to the **Image library** tab and back, or
> refreshing the page, keeps your generated set on screen.

---

## 5. Get your caption (free)

The studio gives you a **prompt**, not the finished caption — so it costs
nothing. To turn it into a caption:

1. Click **📋 Copy prompt** on the event (or weekly) card.
2. Open **claude.ai** or **ChatGPT** (your normal account is fine).
3. **Paste** the prompt and send it.
4. The AI researches NomadGao + The Hotpot House and gives you **3 caption
   options** — pick one, tweak if you like, and post it.

The prompt already includes the event details, the location, the RSVP line, and
the rule of **no hashtags** — so the captions come out on-brand.

---

## 6. Reuse a past week ("Recent weeks")

Scroll down on the form to **Recent weeks** — every week you've generated is
saved there. For any of them:
- **Open posters →** — jump straight back into the studio for that week.
- **Load into form** — pull it back into the form to tweak and re-generate.

---

## 7. Admin: the Image Library

Open the **🗂 Image library · Admin** tab.

- **Brand logos** — the NomadGao and The Hotpot House logos sit on every
  creative. Use **⤓ Replace** to swap one if it ever changes.
- **Event photos** — under each event type, click **Add images** to upload
  past-event photos. The generator pulls a real photo from the matching type as
  the background for that event's poster. **The more good photos you add, the
  better the event posters look.**
  - Hover a photo and click **✕** to remove it.
- **Add event type** — create a new category (it also appears in the form's type
  dropdown).
- **⤓ Download events (Excel)** — export every past event (dates, times,
  location, price, description — no images) to a spreadsheet.

> Photos and logos you upload are saved for everyone and stay there until
> someone deletes them.

---

## 8. Tips for the best output

- **Write a real description.** "Brushes, canvas and a sundowner" beats "Sip &
  Paint". It drives the caption.
- **Add event photos** in the Image Library so each poster uses a real,
  on-theme background.
- **Shuffle** a few times and pick the look you like before downloading.
- **Download both sizes** — square for the feed, story for stories.
- **Pick the right venue** in the Location dropdown — it shows on the poster and
  in the caption.

---

## 9. Quick troubleshooting

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Can't sign in | Your email isn't on the approved list — ask the admin to add it. |
| My event type isn't in the list | Use **＋ Add new type…** in the Event type dropdown. |
| The caption box is just a prompt, not a caption | That's intended — copy it into claude.ai / ChatGPT to get the caption (free). |
| The download takes a few seconds | The image is being rendered at full quality — that's normal. |
| I lost my week | Check **Recent weeks** on the form and click **Open posters →**. |
